Brain


Ấn để chuyển qua tiếng Việt
  • The brain is divided into three main parts: the cerebrum, brainstem, and cerebellum.
  • Cerebrum: It consists of two main parts, the right cerebral hemisphere and the left
    cerebral hemisphere, which are separated by the longitudinal fissure.
    Inside the cerebrum are four cavities containing cerebrospinal fluid called ventricles,
    connected by openings and channels. Cerebrospinal fluid produced by the ventricles helps
    protect and reduce trauma to the brain.
  • Brainstem: It consists of the midbrain, pons, and medulla oblongata.
    The brainstem lies in front of the cerebellum and acts as a relay station,
    transmitting information to other organs in the body.
  • Cerebellum: Located behind the brainstem, below the cerebrum.
    The cerebellum coordinates motor movements, maintains posture, and balances the body.
  • The brain is composed of nerve cells called neurons and supporting cells called glial cells.
  • The brain's functions include stimulation, transmission, sending and receiving nerve impulses.
    It balances the internal environment, supports, nourishes.
